Background

Gege Akutami (芥あくた見み下げ々げ Akutami Gege?) is a Japanese manga artist best known for creating Jujutsu Kaisen and its prequel, Jujutsu Kaisen 0: Jujutsu High.


Staff

Junya Fukuda (福田純也 Fukuda Junya?) (Second Editor)


Awards

Gege Akutami In 2016, Akutami's one-shot Nikai Bongai Barabarujura was nominated for Weekly Shōnen Jump's 11th Gold Future Cup contest. In 2019, his manga Jujutsu Kaisen was nominated for the 65th Shogakukan Manga Award in the shōnen category.Akutami is the 2020 grand prize winner for Jujutsu Kaisen on Mando Kobayashi, Kendo Kobayashi's monthly manga variety show where winners are selected on his personal taste. In 2021, Akutami's Jujutsu Kaisen was listed as a nominee for the 25th Annual Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prize.

Writings

45-Page | One-shot


Kamishiro Sōsa

In 2014, Akutami created Kamishiro Sōsa (神代捜査カミシロソウサ?), a 45 pages one-shot, that was published in the Jump Next 2014 vol. 2 issue.








No.9

The next year, 2015, Akutami created another 43 page one-shot named No.9 (No.9ナンバーナイン?), in the Jump Next 2015 vol. 2 issue. The same year, a new version of No.9 (No.9ナンバーナイン?) one-shot (47 pages) telling a different story, was published in the Weekly Shonen Jump 2015-46 issue.








Nikai Bongai Barabarjura

In 2016, Akutami created a new 47 page one-shot Nikai Bongai Barabarjura (二に界かい梵ぼん骸がい バラバルジュラ?), was published in Weekly Shonen Jump 2016-44 issue. The one-shot was part of Jump's Golden Future Cup as entry #5.









Jujutsu Kaisen 0: Jujutsu High

The following year (2017), Akutami started their first serialization with Jujutsu Kaisen 0: Jujutsu High (東京都立呪術高等専門学校とうきょうとりつじゅじゅつこうとうせんもんがっこう?), in Jump GIGA 2017 vol. 1 issue. The series was meant to be a short one and continued until 2017 vol.4 issue. The series was about a cursed child who entered a magical school in order to free himself of the curse and help others in similar situations.





Jujutsu Kaisen

In 2018, due to the good reception of the Akutami's previous series, a full serialization of the series was granted, starting in Weekly Shonen Jump 2018-14 issue. The series was titled Jujutsu Kaisen (呪術廻戦じゅじゅつかいせん?) and was a direct sequel to their previous work. The story was about a boy who entered a magical school in an attempt to collect and kill all the parts of a powerful cursed spirit.









































Jujutsu Kaisen

Jujutsu Kaisen, created by Gege Akutami, has taken the anime and manga world by storm with its captivating story, dynamic characters, and stunning artwork. In this article, we will explore the tremendous success of Jujutsu Kaisen's manga and its transition from page to screen, analyzing the factors that contributed to its popularity and the impact it has made in the industry(Napier,2023).


The Rise of Jujutsu Kaisen

Jujutsu Kaisen first debuted in Shueisha's Weekly Shonen Jump magazine in March 2018, quickly gaining a dedicated following. The manga's unique blend of supernatural themes, intense battles, and intricate world-building resonated with readers, propelling its popularity to new heights(Napier,2023).


Key Factors Contributing to Success

Engaging Storytelling

Jujutsu Kaisen's manga achieved remarkable success in terms of sales and rankings. According to reputable sources like Oricon and Manga Plus, the series consistently topped the bestseller lists, demonstrating its commercial success and growing demand among readers(Napier,2023).


Dynamic Characters

The manga features a diverse cast of characters, each with their own unique personalities, backstories, and motivations. Fans are drawn to the well-developed characters and their growth throughout the series. Memorable characters like Yuji Itadori, Megumi Fushiguro, and Satoru Gojo have become fan favorites, contributing to the manga's widespread popularity(Napier,2023).


Artwork and Visual Appeal

Gege Akutami's distinct art style plays a significant role in the manga's success. The intricate details, dynamic panel layouts, and powerful action sequences bring the story to life visually. The manga's stunning artwork and designs effectively capture the supernatural elements, captivating readers and enhancing their overall experience(Napier,2023).


Adaptation to Anime

The announcement of Jujutsu Kaisen's anime adaptation in 2020 generated tremendous anticipation among fans. The anime, produced by MAPPA studio, was met with positive reception, garnering praise for its faithfulness to the source material and its exceptional animation quality. The successful transition from page to screen further solidified the franchise's popularity. The anime's release had a significant impact on manga sales, with reports indicating a surge in demand for Jujutsu Kaisen's manga volumes. The anime's success served as a catalyst, introducing new audiences to the series and prompting existing fans to explore the source material(Napier,2023).


Cultural and Industry Impact

Jujutsu Kaisen's manga and anime have gained recognition and acclaim not only in Japan but also internationally. The series has cultivated a strong global fan base, with a significant following in North America, Europe, and other parts of the world. Its popularity has led to the establishment of fan communities, cosplay events, and merchandise sales, showcasing the cultural impact of the franchise.In the industry, Jujutsu Kaisen's success has influenced the perception of dark fantasy manga, paving the way for similar titles to receive attention and recognition. It has also sparked discussions about the manga's themes, character dynamics, and storytelling techniques, further contributing to the growth of the medium as a whole(Napier,2023).


Conclusion

Jujutsu Kaisen's manga has achieved remarkable success, captivating readers with its engaging storytelling, dynamic characters, and stunning artwork. The seamless transition from page to screen through the anime adaptation further solidified its popularity and introduced the series to a wider audience. With its significant cultural and industry impact, Jujutsu Kaisen has firmly established itself as a powerhouse in the world of manga and anime(Napier,2023).
